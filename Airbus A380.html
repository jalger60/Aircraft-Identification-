Airbus A380: The "Superjumbo"

The Airbus A380, the world's largest passenger airliner, is a double-deck, wide-body, four-engine jetliner. It first took to the skies on April 27, 2005, and entered commercial service with Singapore Airlines on October 25, 2007. Production of the aircraft ceased in 2021.

A380-800 Specifications

The A380-800 is the primary passenger model. Its key specifications include:

Seating 525 (3-class) up to 853 (all-economy)
Length 72.7 m (238 ft 6 in)
Wingspan 79.8 m (261 ft 10 in)
Height 24.1 m (79 ft 1 in)
Range 15,200 km (8,200 nmi)
Engines Rolls-Royce Trent 900 or Engine Alliance GP7200

Major Incidents

The A380 has a notable safety record with no fatal accidents. Significant incidents include:

  • Qantas Flight 32 (Nov 4, 2010): Uncontained engine failure after takeoff from Singapore. The aircraft landed safely with no injuries.
  • Air France Flight 66 (Sep 30, 2017): Uncontained engine failure over Greenland. The aircraft diverted and landed safely.
  • JFK Ground Collision (Apr 14, 2011): An Air France A380 clipped the tail of a regional jet while taxiing. No injuries were reported.

Variants

While the A380-800 was the only passenger version produced, several other variants were planned:

  • A380F (Freighter): This cargo version was planned and received orders, but the project was ultimately canceled.
  • A380-900: A stretched concept that could have seated around 650 passengers but was never launched.
  • Other Concepts: Airbus also explored shortened and extended-range versions, but these did not move into production.
